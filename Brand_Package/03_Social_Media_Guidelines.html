Brand Voice & Personality

Core Message: Brasa Roja is where family tradition meets the art of fire-cooked cuisine. Every post should evoke warmth, authenticity, and the sensory experience of our kitchen.

We Are...

  • Warm - Like gathering around a fire
  • Authentic - Real recipes, real stories
  • Passionate - About fire-cooked food
  • Family-oriented - Everyone belongs
  • Premium but accessible - Quality without pretension

We Are Not...

  • Stuffy or overly formal
  • Trendy for trend's sake
  • Discount-focused or cheap
  • Cold or corporate
  • Pushy or salesy

Visual Guidelines

Photography Style

  • Lighting: Warm, golden tones (match the flame aesthetic)
  • Mood: Dramatic but inviting (dark backgrounds, spot-lit food)
  • Action: Capture flames, steam, motion when possible
  • Angles: 45° overhead for plates, eye-level for atmosphere
  • Props: Cast iron, wood boards, rustic elements

Color Overlay Guidelines

  • Use warm color grading (orange/amber tones)
  • Increase warmth by 10-15% in editing
  • Boost shadows slightly for richness
  • Never use cool/blue filters

Brand Colors for Graphics

Brasa Gold - #C9882B (headlines, accents)
Roja Red - #C63333 (primary accent)
Charcoal - #2D2D2D (backgrounds)
Crema - #F5EFE0 (light backgrounds)

Font Usage in Graphics

  • Headlines: Cinzel Bold (or similar serif)
  • Body text: Lora or system sans-serif
  • Accent: Parisienne (tagline only)

Do's and Don'ts

DO

  • Show real food, real flames, real people
  • Use warm, inviting language
  • Respond to every comment within 24 hours
  • Share user-generated content (with permission)
  • Post consistently (minimum 4x/week)
  • Use Stories daily for real-time content
  • Highlight team members by name
  • Share the "why" behind dishes

DON'T

  • Use stock photos (always real content)
  • Post blurry or poorly lit images
  • Engage in negative exchanges
  • Over-promote or be "salesy"
  • Use blue/cold filters
  • Ignore customer feedback
  • Post without proofreading
  • Use trending sounds that don't fit the brand

Influencer & Collaboration Guidelines

When Working With Food Influencers:
  • Prioritize local food bloggers with 5K-50K engaged followers over large accounts
  • Require proper disclosure (#ad #sponsored #gifted)
  • Provide brand assets and key messaging points
  • Request approval on posts before publishing (when possible)
  • Always offer a full experience, not just free food
  • Track results: engagement rate, website traffic, reservations

Crisis Management Protocol

Situation Response Time Action
Negative review (1-2 stars) Within 4 hours Acknowledge, apologize, offer to discuss privately
Food safety complaint Immediately Take offline immediately, investigate, escalate to management
Viral negative post Within 1 hour Do not delete, respond professionally, escalate to ownership
Misinformation about restaurant Within 2 hours Politely correct with facts, provide proof if needed
Golden Rule: Never delete negative comments unless they contain profanity, threats, or spam. Deleting valid criticism makes the situation worse.
